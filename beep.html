Beep, Beep... Dead

Work

Group project, where the task was to create a product that would encourage, or be used within, a physical activity. It resulted in a prototype for a phone application that were to be used for playing Paintball. The only output from the application itself while actually playing were vibrations and sound, that would alert the player about the boundaries of the ever-shrinking playing field. The prototype was accompanied by a video showing off the concept.

Playing the Game

The playing field consists of X amount of zones, whos space and boundaries are based on GPS coordinates. For the players on the playing field there are no teams, instead it is everyone against everyone, where the winner is the sole survivor. To draw the players closer to each other, and force them into combat, one by one the zones closes after X amount of time, gradually making the playing field smaller. The players, themselves tracked by GPS, are alerted of their presence in a closed zone by two types of outputs, vibrations and sounds. A vibration/sound in intervals in the right direction, towards an open zone, and a constant vibration/sound in the wrong direction.
