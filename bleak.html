"B.Leak" was my MSc design project that resulted in a thesis named Informing Users About Fingerprinting. In this context, fingerprinting is the web tracking technique in which bits and pieces of information about a user and their device create a digital fingerprint. During my study, I explored ways to inform users and make them aware of this kind of web tracking. I did this to find out: what key aspects of visual aesthetics that affected the users' experience when being informed; and to what extent differences in technological interest and knowledge affected the reception of the information.

The thesis is available to read at Digitala Vetenskapliga Arkivet.

    The first version of the design project was in the form of a browser extension for the Google Chrome web browser. It was designed and programmed to display information about the user's devices. However, during user tests, aspects of the design itself took focus rather than the content it display, and thus deviated from the test's purpose. Hence a later second version.

    The second version took the form of a prototyped page made to look and feel like the internal settings pages of the Google Chrome web browser. As if the user would be able to find it from the browser's own menu. With a neutral and familiar visual look, the idea was to steer the focus back to the actual content.

Further, it also showed a way for how web browsers can inform its users on topics such as Fingerprinting, and the aspects users valued from it. Additionally it gave insights on how technological interest and knowledge affects users approach and interactions with software, and the implications this brings that could be taken in consideration by designers and developers for future browser related functionality implementations.
